Live test — over hot-zone heat

The exact failure mode of the old amber chevrons: the worst-case background is amber heat density along a corridor, with red high-density bursts. The white-fill alert glyphs survive; the muted gray glyphs correctly recede. Pulse rings + heading rotation are previewed below.

Rationale

The problem. The old chevrons rendered in --alert amber — the same hue as the hot-zone heatmap. Riders were losing the icons against the warm density along I-5/I-405, exactly where they matter most. Worse, all four roles looked identical: a WSP Cessna read the same as a SAR Huey, even though those demand opposite responses.

Color strategy. White fill, amber outline (alert variants). White-on-amber-stroke is air-traffic-control display convention: white aircraft on a colored radar field. We borrow that visual grammar — it survives the amber heat (high-contrast white against amber halo), reads as "aircraft on a radar," and ties back to brand via stroke without sharing the heat's hue. Non-alert variants drop to muted gray with no stroke: present, but visually deprioritized.

A small amber signal blip sits at the nose of alert variants only — it echoes the brand glyph's orbit blip and acts as a heading cue (the blip is always "ahead").

The campaign-hat tie. The brand mark is the trooper's flat-brim campaign hat. We wanted a nod without making the icons decorative. Decision: subtle hat-brim profile in the cockpit center mass, read top-down — the fuselage in both families flares slightly outward at the cockpit shoulders before tapering aft. At 16px the bulge is invisible; the icon just reads "plane" or "helicopter." That is the goal — the brand tie surfaces at 40px+, and we explicitly rejected "hat with wings" for the same reason: mixing the mark and the data icon would harm 16px legibility.

Heading. Glyphs are drawn facing north (up). Consumer applies transform: rotate(${"`${track}deg`"}). Do not bake rotation into the path data.

Pulse ring. The 1.6s ease-in-out 0.4 → 1.0 ring already shipping continues to apply. Wrap the icon in a container; pulse the ring (in fill color) on the container; rotate the icon (not the container) by track. This keeps the pulse circular regardless of bearing — see the live demo above.

Output format. Pure static SVG. brand/aircraft-glyphs.js exports window.SS_AIRCRAFT with svg(role, {size}), path-only exports for embedding inside your own SVG, the role table, and color tokens. No animations baked in, no JS required at runtime.
